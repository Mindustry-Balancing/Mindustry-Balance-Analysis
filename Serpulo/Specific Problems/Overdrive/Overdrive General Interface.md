# Overdrive

## Quick Introduction
Overdrive as of writing is a simple block.
- It is primarily a plastanium tech option, that eventually becomes a phase-fabric tech option (phase-overdrive), and a phase-surge tech option (overdrive dome).
- Its current stats are 50/125/150, representing the boost values of each.
- What it does is ramp up both the production speed and consumption speed of all blocks inside the radius.
- The consumption of the overdrive itself is fixed.

The goal here is to make decisions interesting which coincided by making them harder to decide. This is what it means for something to be balanced. Overdrive is no exception.

## Analysis

The main identified purpose of overdrive is scaling and space effeciency. To explain, its best first I move onto a related object, **erekir heat**.
- The erekir heat system is the main method of scaling. It happens because as the methods of producing heat improves in maintainence at the cost of upfront cost, the space that such factories take up goes lower and lower. Compare electric heated carbide crucible vs phase heated and see how much reduced the space is.
- In a similar vein, overdrive is the main method at which each "factory" occupies less space, and produces much more quickly.
- However, unlike the erekir heat system, overdrive nearly affects every aspect: Ore-Production, Factories, Turrets, Menders, and even Power Production.

Now that the purpose has been identitfied, let's analyze further to the specific dynamics this block has.

### Upfront and Production Cost

As it stands, the current overdrive, phase-overdrive, and overdrive dome, are area of effect with fixed consumption. If the production cost is made signficantly meaningful through higher values, what may happen is that the players are more incentivised to keep as many buildings (existing and future) within radius.
- If the increased packing of buildings is unintentional or disfavored due to discouraging exploration and utilization of spaces beyond the point, nerfs to the intensity of the fixed consumption is not preferable.

Upfront Cost meanwhile tells about the tech and accesbility of the block itself. Changes on this regard is more due to time and delay. It can be thought as a production cost for the first "x" minutes and remains absent afterwards.
- In general, nerfs along this line correspond to delays and waiting until current production can reasonably get it.
- By analyzing the return on investment, it tells you how much production is required within the region to pay itself back off.
