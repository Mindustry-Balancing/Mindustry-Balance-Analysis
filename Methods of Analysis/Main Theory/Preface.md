# Preface

These essays and paragraphs of text analysing balancing for a video game titled Mindustry is not really something people see everyday. I'm Someone's Shadow _(call me Shade for short)_ and this is a passion project for my love of the game really. I've been in the discord's balancing channel for at least more than a year as I'm currently writing this in 2026, and I've mostly developed a lot of the thinking and theory on how to even give some sense of objectivity to an otherwise subjective sense on what makes something balanced. When one endures intense pressure where everyone cares with their own perspective and experience in a complex and multifaceted game such as Mindustry, it really brings out the best on what one could do. 

What this series is really for is to encapusalte and even pass on the thinking and effort I've done for well attempting to balance this game. Surprisingly, a lot of the concepts here can transfer to other similar games and even help form a more objective sense on how to balance. While it is preferable to make a game feels right, it may not feel right to others as well and one needs to know why? Also, among the different variables and mechanics, which ones to adjust, remove, and add to make the desired outcome true? How can one make sure that every piece of content that you have would be experienced and given meaning to? How to deal the community as they have varying opinions and perspectives on something that is multifaceted as this game?

These questions, among many, were the main drive on why I would want to share a lot of the technicallities and experiences. I just want my salvo to feel nice without being broken. I'd want the coolant system to actually be good. I'd want nova to be meaningful in its healing capabilities. I've experienced many mental breakdowns as I had to debate out why I want a change as many view points clash, analogous to real life politics.

For such a series of analysis were born out of the desire to understand, to learn, to satisfy curiousity, to gain knowledge, and to put all theory into practice into making the game a better place. 

This has been the most meaningful activity I've had at a time were I struggled to know who I was, and is a main expression and appreciation to game developers and researchers in one repository!

- Someone's Shadow
